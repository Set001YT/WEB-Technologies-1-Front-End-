About Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and gravity. He developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics alongside quantum mechanics. His mass-energy equivalence formula E=mc¬≤ has become the world's most famous equation. Einstein's groundbreaking work earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 and established him as one of the greatest scientists in human history.
